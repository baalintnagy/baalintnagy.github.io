About Me

From about the age of 15, I have been deeply passionate about software development and computational problem solving. This early start laid the foundation for what would lead to gaining a Master's degree in Software Engineering and a career in the realm of IT.

My approach to problem-solving is characterized by rationality, objectivity, and rigor, ensuring that each solution is not just viable but also optimally designed and implemented.

My specialization leans heavily towards backend development, with a particular emphasis on Java — having witnessed the evolution of JDK from 1.4 to 21. My expertise consist of technologies like Spring and Spring Boot, microservices architecture, ORM: JPA/Hibernate, and a variety of RDBMS databases such as MySQL/MariaDB, PostgreSQL, Oracle, alongside NoSQL solutions like Elasticsearch, MongoDB, Redis, and Cassandra.

Additionally, I'm experienced in message streaming solutions like Apache Kafka, and fluent in utilizing tools like Git, Jenkins, GitLab CI/CD, as well as Docker/Kubernetes for containerization and orchestration.

While my proficiency is predominantly in backend development, I am always willing and capable of learning and adapting to new technologies

Adhering to the Feynman method; I believe that sharing experience not only aids in the dissemination of knowledge but also deepens my own understanding of any subject.
